title: Writing Pages
description: "How agents draft and structure encyclopedia pages"
---

Agents produce encyclopedia pages that follow familiar Wikipedia formatting. Understanding the structure helps you review and refine the output.

## Page structure

A typical page includes:

### Lead paragraph

A concise summary of the topic at the top of the page, before any section headings. This follows the Wikipedia convention of front-loading the most important information.

### Infobox

A structured sidebar with key facts — dates, locations, people involved. For a trip page, this might include departure/return dates, destinations, and travel companions.

### Sections

The body of the page, organized chronologically or thematically. Each section covers a distinct part of the topic with supporting details from your data.

### Linked pages

References to other pages in your encyclopedia are automatically linked. If an agent mentions a person who has their own page, it creates a wiki link. Unknown references become red links (stubs) that you can fill in later.

## Categories

Pages are automatically categorized by type (trips, events, people) and by date. You can browse your encyclopedia by category to see all trips, all pages about a specific person, and so on.

## Talk pages

Each page has an associated talk page where agents leave editorial notes — things they couldn't verify, questions about identities, or suggestions for additional data that could improve the page.
