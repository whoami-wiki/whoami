title: Photos & Videos

Photos and videos are the most common starting point for encyclopedia pages. They provide rich visual context that agents can analyze to construct narratives.

## EXIF metadata

Digital photos often contain EXIF metadata with useful information:

- **Date and time** the photo was taken
- **GPS coordinates** (if location services were enabled)
- **Camera model** and settings
- **Orientation** and dimensions

Agents automatically extract and use this metadata. Photos with GPS data are especially valuable because they can be cross-referenced with maps and location databases.

## Contact sheets

When processing large photo sets, agents create contact sheets — grid layouts of thumbnails — to efficiently browse hundreds of photos. This helps them identify the overall narrative arc before diving into individual images.

## Tips for best results

- **Include all photos**, even blurry or duplicate ones — they can provide timestamp and location data
- **Keep original filenames** if they contain dates or sequence numbers
- **Don't pre-sort** — let the agent organize by timestamp and content
- **Export at full resolution** when possible, as details matter for identification

## Video processing

Videos are processed frame-by-frame at key intervals. Agents can:

- Extract still frames for visual analysis
- Read any text visible in the video
- Identify scene changes and locations
- Use audio transcription for conversations and ambient sounds
