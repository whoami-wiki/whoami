whoami.wiki is your personal encyclopedia, written by AI agents. It uses [MediaWiki](https://www.mediawiki.org/wiki/MediaWiki) as its foundation to turn your scattered digital data — photos, messages, transactions, location history — into structured, interconnected encyclopedia pages about your life.

<Features />

## How it works

You bring your data exports (photos, chat logs, bank statements, location timelines) and point AI agents at them. The agents browse through your data, cross-reference details across sources, and draft encyclopedia pages using Wikipedia conventions — infoboxes, citations, linked pages, categories, and talk pages.

## Why MediaWiki?

Language models already understand Wikipedia conventions deeply from their training data. The encyclopedia format provides a proven structure for organizing knowledge:

- **Infoboxes** summarize key facts at a glance
- **Linked pages** connect people, places, and events across your life
- **Categories** group pages by theme
- **Talk pages** track editorial notes and clarifications
- **Revision history** shows how pages evolve as new data comes in

A page about your grandmother's wedding works the same way as a page about a royal wedding. A page about your best friend works the same way as a page about a public figure.

## What you can build

People have used whoami.wiki to document:

- Family history from old photos and oral interviews
- Travel journals from photos, location data, and bank transactions
- Friendship arcs from years of messages and voice notes
- Gaming histories from server backups and chat logs
- Life timelines from social media archives

## Open source

whoami.wiki is fully open source. Your encyclopedia runs on your machine, your data stays with you, and any model can read and write to it.
