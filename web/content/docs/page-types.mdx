Every piece of content has exactly one canonical home. Pages are organized by namespace and type, each with specific structure, editorial conventions, and infobox templates.

For namespace details, see [Namespaces](/docs/namespaces). For citation conventions, see [Citation System](/docs/citation-system).

## Area

**Namespace**: Main (e.g. `Area 03 — Primary Clarifiers`)

The encyclopedic article about a process area or geographic zone of the project. Area pages serve as hubs linking drawings, specifications, equipment, and construction documents that affect that area.

**Standard sections:**

- Lead paragraph — what the area is, its function, key dimensions
- Discipline sections (Structural, Mechanical, Electrical, I&C, Civil) — at least two
- Connected Systems — upstream/downstream relationships
- Construction Requirements — sequencing, special procedures
- References and categories

**What belongs here:**

- Design parameters with citations to specific drawings and spec paragraphs
- Cross-references to equipment, drawing, and spec pages
- Construction sequencing requirements
- Verification status on the talk page

**Lead paragraph convention:**

> Area 03 encompasses the primary clarifier complex consisting of four 120-foot diameter circular clarifiers with associated sludge collection and scum removal systems. The clarifiers receive raw wastewater from the headworks via a 48-inch reinforced concrete influent pipe.

## Equipment

**Namespace**: Main (e.g. `PS-301A`)

A page for a specific piece of equipment identified by its tag number. Includes design parameters, location, electrical, controls, and submittal status.

**Key fields:** tag number, capacity/design parameters, area link, spec section link, submittal status.

## Drawing

**Namespace**: Drawing (e.g. `Drawing:C-301`)

A structured analysis of a single contract drawing. Follows an observations-before-interpretation protocol.

**Standard sections:**

1. Title Block — tabulated drawing metadata
2. Observations — physical observations without interpretation
3. Dimensions and Elevations — tabulated key dimensions and elevations
4. Material and Specification Callouts — materials referenced on the drawing
5. Cross-References — links to other drawings with verification status
6. Annotations and Notes — general notes and special instructions
7. Engineering Interpretation — analysis and significance (only after observations)

**Key principle:** Observations come before interpretation. Record what you see on the drawing before analyzing what it means. This prevents confirmation bias and ensures nothing is missed.

## Spec

**Namespace**: Spec (e.g. `Spec:03 30 00`)

A specification section page following CSI MasterFormat numbering. Preserves the Part 1/2/3 structure and uses `{{Verbatim}}` blocks for exact contract language.

**Standard sections:**

- Part 1 — General (scope, submittals, quality assurance)
- Part 2 — Products (materials, equipment, fabrication)
- Part 3 — Execution (preparation, installation, testing)
- Active Modifications — table of RFIs/changes affecting this section

**Key features:**

- Paragraph numbering preserved (e.g., `=== 2.1 Concrete Materials ===`)
- `{{Verbatim}}` template for exact contract language that may matter for scope or change arguments
- Submittal requirements extracted and linked to the Submittal Log
- Areas field linking to affected process areas

## Construction document

**Namespace**: Construction (e.g. `Construction:RFI-042`)

A unified page type for RFIs, submittals, field directives, change orders, and other construction-phase documents. The infobox `type` field distinguishes between document types.

**Standard sections by type:**

- **RFI**: Question, Response, Pages Updated
- **Submittal**: Submittal Contents, Review Comments, Pages Updated
- **Field Directive**: Directive, Pages Updated

**Pages Updated** is critical — it records which wiki pages were modified as a result of this document, maintaining the traceability chain.

## Issue

**Namespace**: Issue (e.g. `Issue:003`)

A project-level issue tracking drawing conflicts, spec ambiguities, missing information, or risk items discovered during document analysis.

**Standard sections:**

- Description — what was found and where
- Impact — schedule, quality, cost, environmental consequences
- Recommended Action — specific steps to resolve
- Resolution — outcome (or "Pending")

**Severity levels:** Critical, High, Moderate, Low

Issues are distinct from talk page Active Gaps. Issues are project-level problems requiring action (e.g., missing dewatering spec). Active Gaps are page-level uncertainties (e.g., unconfirmed vendor for a mechanism).
