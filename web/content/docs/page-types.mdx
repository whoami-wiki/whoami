---
title: Page Types
description: "The five page types in a whoami.wiki encyclopedia"
---

whoami.wiki uses five page types to organize your encyclopedia. Each type has its own structure and purpose.

## Person

A biographical page about someone in your life. Includes an infobox with key facts (name, relationship, birth date), a lead paragraph, and chronological sections covering your shared history.

**When to use**: Any individual who appears meaningfully in your data — family members, close friends, colleagues, recurring acquaintances.

**Example**: A page about your college roommate, covering how you met, things you did together, and where they are now, all cited from photos, messages, and shared memories.

## Episode

A narrative page about a specific event or period. Covers a bounded experience with a beginning and end — a trip, a move, a project, a celebration.

**When to use**: Any distinct event or time-bounded experience that forms a coherent story.

**Example**: A page about a two-week trip to Japan, structured chronologically with sections for each city, citing photos, location data, and bank transactions.

## Talk

A page built from a conversation or series of conversations. Captures the substance of what was discussed, who was involved, and the context around it.

**When to use**: Significant conversations from messages, voice notes, or interviews — anything where the dialogue itself is the primary content.

**Example**: A page reconstructing a family group chat during a holiday, capturing the planning, jokes, and logistics from WhatsApp exports.

## Reflection

An introspective page synthesized from personal data. Draws patterns and themes across multiple sources to articulate something about who you are or how you've changed.

**When to use**: When the interesting story isn't a single event but a pattern across time — how a relationship evolved, how your interests shifted, what a year meant to you.

**Example**: A page about your evolving relationship with cooking, tracing from early takeout receipts through recipe messages to photos of elaborate meals.

## Task

An operational page tracking something that needs to be done or investigated. Used by agents to coordinate work — pages that need more data, citations that need verification, topics that should be split into separate pages.

**When to use**: Created automatically by agents to track editorial work. You can also create tasks manually to direct agent attention.

**Example**: A task page noting that three person pages reference "the lake house" and suggesting it deserves its own episode page with location data from 2019.
