The project wiki agents work with construction contract documents. The more document types you provide, the richer and more cross-referenced your wiki pages become.

## Contract drawings

The foundation of the project wiki. Agents analyze drawings following a structured observations-before-interpretation protocol, extracting dimensions, elevations, materials, cross-references, and design intent.

**Formats**: PDF (individual sheets or split volumes), PNG, TIFF, JPEG

Drawings are organized by discipline prefix:
- **C** — Civil (site plans, grading, piping)
- **S** — Structural (foundations, framing, details)
- **M** — Mechanical (equipment plans, piping, HVAC)
- **E** — Electrical (single lines, power distribution, lighting)
- **I** — Instrumentation (P&IDs, control diagrams)
- **A** — Architectural (floor plans, elevations, details)

## Specifications

Technical specifications organized by CSI MasterFormat (XX XX XX). Agents extract Part 1/2/3 structure, preserve verbatim contract language, and identify submittal requirements.

**Formats**: PDF, Word documents

Key divisions commonly processed:
- **Division 01** — General Requirements (procedures, standards, quality control)
- **Division 03** — Concrete
- **Division 31** — Earthwork
- **Division 33** — Utilities
- **Division 43** — Process equipment

## RFIs (Requests for Information)

Formal questions and engineer responses that clarify or modify contract requirements. Agents trace each RFI back to the affected drawings and specifications, and update all wiki pages in the traceability chain.

## Submittals

Manufacturer data, shop drawings, mix designs, and other contractor-provided documents submitted for engineer review. Agents track submittal status and update equipment and spec pages when submittals are approved.

## Geotechnical reports

Subsurface investigation reports providing soil conditions, groundwater elevations, bearing capacities, and construction recommendations. Agents cross-reference geotechnical data with structural foundations and earthwork specifications.

**Formats**: PDF

## Field directives and change orders

Documents that modify the contract scope, schedule, or cost. Agents track which specifications and drawings are affected and update the wiki accordingly.

## How agents cross-reference

When given multiple document types, agents combine them to build a complete picture. An agent processing an area might:

1. Use **drawings** for physical layout, dimensions, and elevations
2. Use **specifications** for material requirements, testing criteria, and procedures
3. Use **RFI responses** to resolve conflicts between drawings and specs
4. Use **submittals** to update equipment pages with approved manufacturer data
5. Use **geotechnical reports** to verify foundation design assumptions

The more document types overlap for a given area, the more connections agents can make — and the more issues they can identify before construction begins.
