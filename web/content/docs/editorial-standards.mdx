---
title: Editorial Standards
description: "Guidelines for tone, structure, and quality in wiki content"
---

These standards guide how pages should be written — both by AI agents and human contributors. The goal is a consistent, readable encyclopedia that treats personal history with the same rigor as any other subject.

## Tone

Pages are written in **neutral, encyclopedic prose**. The tone is informative and third-person, not sentimental or editorializing.

- Write "Smith traveled to Tokyo in March 2019" not "I had an amazing trip to Tokyo"
- State facts and let the reader draw conclusions
- Use past tense for events, present tense for ongoing states

## Structure

Every page should have:

- **Lead paragraph** — a concise summary of the topic before any section headings, front-loading the most important information
- **Infobox** — a structured sidebar with key facts (dates, locations, people involved)
- **Sections** — the body, organized chronologically or thematically with clear headings
- **Citations** — references linking claims back to source data
- **Categories** — tags grouping the page by type and theme

## Sourcing

All factual claims should be cited. A citation links a statement to a specific piece of source data in the archive — a photo, a message, a transaction, a voice note.

- Prefer primary sources (the actual photo, message, or document) over inferred information
- When information is inferred or uncertain, note it on the talk page
- Never fabricate details that aren't supported by the data

## Handling incomplete data

Not every page will have complete information. When data is missing:

- State what is known and leave gaps rather than speculating
- Use red links for people and places that don't have their own page yet
- Add notes on the talk page about what additional data could improve the page

## Quality expectations

A good page:

- Can be understood by someone with no prior context
- Has at least one citation per major claim
- Links to related pages in the encyclopedia
- Is categorized correctly by type and theme
- Has a talk page with any editorial notes or open questions
