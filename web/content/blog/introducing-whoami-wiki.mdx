We're excited to introduce **whoami.wiki** — your personal encyclopedia, written by agents.

## What is whoami.wiki?

whoami.wiki is a new kind of personal knowledge tool. It watches how you work, learns what matters to you, and builds a living encyclopedia about *you* — your projects, your preferences, your workflows.

Think of it as a second brain that actually keeps up with your first one.

## Why we built this

We've all been there: searching through old notes, trying to remember that terminal command, or explaining the same context to a new tool for the hundredth time. Your knowledge is scattered across documents, chat logs, and your own memory.

whoami.wiki brings it all together. Agents observe your work, extract the important bits, and organize them into wiki pages that stay current as you evolve.

## How it works

1. **Install the CLI or desktop app** — it runs quietly in the background
2. **Work as you normally do** — whoami.wiki watches and learns
3. **Your wiki builds itself** — structured pages appear about your tools, projects, and habits
4. **Query it anytime** — ask questions and get answers grounded in your own context

## What's next

This is just the beginning. We're working on deeper integrations, collaborative wikis, and smarter agents that can connect dots you didn't even know existed.
